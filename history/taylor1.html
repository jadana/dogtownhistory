TAYLOR CITY BELT LINE RAILROAD

Tonight I received a note from Joe Cleghorn giving me the url of a huge map of St. Louis. The map says it was from 1912, but as soon as you look at it your realize it is 1904, this for two reasons: first you can see the structure of the World's Fair in Forest Park (I trimmed the map down to just Forest Park and most of Dogtown); second, the Taylor City Belt Line Railroad is there!

Only a few of you on this list have been with me since the beginning. Those who have know of my nearly mania about that railroad. It was clearly on the map, but tonight I learned a brand new trick: how to draw on a map. So, in RED I outlined the path of the Taylor City Belt Line from Manchester, where it splits off from the Mo Pac and Frisco lines, and runs into Forest Park, then off to the left to warehouse area.

First, let's get the map up:

On the north of the map is the northern edge of Forest Park and the main entrance to the Fair. One the south of the map is Manchester. You can see Richmond Heights to the west and the eastern border I have is Kingshighway.

Within two-three weeks at most I will really begin my count-down reasearch up at the Mercantile library on this railroad. They have one of the largest collections of railroad materials in existence. I hope to have a detailed story up no later than mid-July if all goes well. What's here is just a taste, a whetting of the appetite and a chance for me to show off my brand new tool I just learned tonight, how to draw on a photo!! I've got a million uses for that.

Okay, 1:15 AM. Time for a bit of sleep. I still have to get this loaded to my web site, hmmmmm. By the way, I'm not putting this on where anyone can find it. You'll need the special url I'm leaving for you by e-mail since this is just a taste. You can't get to it from my web site.

I'll pull it down soon when I begin the "real" story.
