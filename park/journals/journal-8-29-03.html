BOB CORBETT'S FOREST PARK JOURNALS

Rain, walking, biking in the park

August 29 2003
By Bob Corbett

I just got back from a very long walk in the Forest Park today, my longest walk in probably three years or more. It is so nice to be able to walk again.

But, I'm dripping. I had planned this to be a lovely walk in the rain, cool and alone since no one would be in the park. I wore shorts, but also wore my light weight rain coat. However, it didn't rain, not at all. It had been raining all morning, at times really hard and the weather forecast was for rain all day including thunderstorms, which I didn't want.

Since it didn't rain it was very hot and muggy and I am dripping.

However, I was nearly alone. I walked for nearly two hours, maybe longer, and only ran into three other single walkers and saw one biker and passed a family of three on bikes. That was it. Otherwise I had the park to myself. It was very quiet and reflective and I adored it.

For the first time the ENTIRE circle of the Grand Basin was open without any fences or yellow or orange tape. It is now open. The concrete walk way I described before is open, and goes the entire circle round the basin from the bottom of Art Hill, over the bridges at each end of the basin, and out to the golf course. It is so lovely.

That's where I ran into all the walkers and the one solo biker (who was on very antique bike, must have been from the 1930s at least).

The landscaping isn't finished and it is clear they are doing more. The circle of paved path (concrete, not blacktop) at the bottom of Art Hill has about a 15 yard space between it and the water, so that will certainly be landscaped, I would guess with grass. On the sides of the basin it appears there will be extensive flower beds.

I then walked out from the basin along the brand new path to the zoo, and then up to the Muny and on to the Union Ave. entrance to the park, then back. I just couldn't stop walking is was so quiet, and while very hot and muggy I was just loving it.

New trails are being cut all over the park. Part of that is because for the entire 6.2 miles of the main circular bike trail, there will be a companion trail for walkers and joggers. So there will be two trails, only a few feet apart, one for bikers and roller bladers and the other for joggers and walkers. Very nice. But in addition to that there are still new paved trails springing up all over, like the one from the Grand Basin to the zoo.

What a delightful few hours. I would still have preferred to have been walking in pouring rain without lightening! Or at least a strong drizzle.
