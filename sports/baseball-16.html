ST. JAMES MEN'S BASEBALL IN 1916

Thanks to Dorothy (Malone) Nollau for this photo.

Names on the back of the photo do not indicate order of people on the photo:

First three part of torn area, can't read. Then: Vincent Malone, Fieselman, Dan Prendible, Haemi, Schneider, Lisiton, Wagner, McCann, Ritchell

Only Vicent Malone and Dan Prindible match up with any students who graduated from 1913 through 1918.

Dorothy (Malone) Nollau adds a bit of color to the photo with these comments in a note she sent with the photo (which she donated to our archives:

My dad was very much into sports... My dad played handball on Forest Park courts and was very good. He also played softball and bowling. He was a 'leftie.' He and my mom played tennis every week and took my brother and me along. He traveled to Jeff City with a football team to play the inmates. I loved telling people my dad went to jail! He was a national commissioner for the Municiple Athletic Association, and their softball commissioner for many years.... On fall Sundays he made the rounds to soccer games in many parks and I tagged along. When we arrived home at dinner time we were blue from the cold. Dad got a short of whiskey and I got hot chocolate!

Thanks to Dorothy for the great note!
